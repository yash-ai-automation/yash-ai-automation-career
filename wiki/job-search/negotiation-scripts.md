# Negotiation Scripts

Ready-to-use scripts for salary discussions, geographic discount pushback, below-target responses, and competing offer leverage. Tailored to Yash Anghan's profile and [[compensation]] targets.

## Script 1: Initial Salary Expectations Question

**When asked**: "What are your salary expectations?"

> "Based on my research into the Toronto market for AI/automation roles, and considering my 6+ years of enterprise experience including production AI systems at Morningstar, I'm targeting $100,000 to $120,000 CAD. I'm open to discussing the total compensation structure -- I value the role and team fit alongside compensation."

**Key points**:
- Anchors at the target range, not the minimum
- References specific experience to justify the range
- Signals flexibility on structure without caving on base
- Does not say "negotiable" directly -- says "open to discussing structure"

## Script 2: Geographic Discount Pushback

**When they say**: "We typically pay less for Toronto than our US offices" or "This role is benchmarked to a lower-cost market."

> "I understand compensation varies by market. That said, the Toronto AI talent market is quite competitive right now -- companies like [name comparable employers] are actively hiring at this range. My experience building production AI systems processing 25K+ daily requests and saving businesses $43K+ annually through automation represents concrete value regardless of geography. I'd love to find a number that reflects the impact I'll bring."

**Key points**:
- Acknowledges their point without agreeing to a discount
- Redirects to market data and value delivered
- Uses specific metrics from [[linkedin-profile]] as proof points
- Ends with collaborative framing

## Script 3: Below-Target Offer Response

**When the offer comes in below $100K** (but above [[deal-breakers]] floor of $90K):

> "Thank you for the offer -- I'm genuinely excited about the role and the team. The base of [amount] is a bit below the range I was targeting based on my market research and the scope of this position. Given my background in production AI at Morningstar and enterprise systems at Bell handling 45K daily transactions, I was hoping we could explore getting closer to the $110K-$115K range. Is there flexibility on the base, or could we look at a signing bonus or accelerated review?"

**Key points**:
- Expresses genuine enthusiasm first
- Does not reject -- asks to explore
- Cites specific experience to anchor value
- Offers alternative compensation levers (signing bonus, accelerated review)
- Names a specific counter-range

## Script 4: Competing Offer Leverage

**When you have another offer or active interviews**:

> "I want to be transparent -- I'm also in discussions with [other company/companies] for a similar role, and I've received [an offer / strong interest]. Your team and mission are my preference, which is why I'm having this conversation. To make this work, I'd need the compensation to be competitive with what I'm seeing elsewhere. Can we discuss how to bridge that gap?"

**Key points**:
- Honest without being confrontational
- Positions their company as the preferred choice
- Creates urgency without ultimatums
- Opens the door for them to ask what they need to beat

## Script 5: "Open to Negotiation" Form Field

**When ATS form asks "Are you open to discussing compensation?"**:

> **Answer**: Yes

Do not elaborate in the form. Save the negotiation for live conversations.

## General Negotiation Principles

1. **Never name the minimum**. The $90K floor from [[deal-breakers]] is internal only
2. **Lead with value, not need**. Cite metrics from [[morningstar-software-engineer]] and [[bell-full-stack-developer]]
3. **Negotiate base first**, then structure (equity, bonus, benefits)
4. **Get it in writing** before accepting verbally
5. **Be willing to walk away** if below $90K -- see [[deal-breakers]]
