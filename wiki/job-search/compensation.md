## Market Context (Toronto AI Roles, 2025-2026)

AI Engineer and ML Engineer roles in Toronto typically range:
- **Junior (0-2 years)**: $70K-$90K CAD
- **Mid-level (3-5 years)**: $90K-$120K CAD
- **Senior (5-8 years)**: $110K-$150K CAD
- **Staff/Lead (8+ years)**: $140K-$180K+ CAD

Yash's 6+ years of experience with a mix of enterprise and AI positions him in the mid-to-senior range. The $100K-$120K target is competitive and realistic.

## Compensation Flexibility

Yash is flexible on structure:
- **Base salary**: Primary concern -- target $100K-$120K
- **Equity/stock options**: Open, especially at Series A+ companies (see [[form-fill-data]])
- **Signing bonus**: Nice-to-have, not a deal-breaker
- **Benefits**: Standard Canadian benefits expected (health, dental, RRSP matching)
- **Learning budget**: Valued but not a negotiation lever

## Below-Target Scenarios

If an offer comes in below $100K:
- Below $90K: Hard [[deal-breakers]] -- do not proceed unless exceptional circumstances
- $90K-$100K: Negotiate using [[negotiation-scripts]]. Consider total compensation (equity, benefits, growth potential)
- If geographic discount is cited for Toronto vs. US: Push back -- Toronto AI market is competitive

## Above-Target Scenarios

If an offer exceeds $120K:
- Likely a senior-level or well-funded startup role
- Evaluate for scope creep -- ensure the role matches [[target-roles]] and is not Director-level in disguise

## Salary Research Sources

When evaluating a specific offer's compensation:
- Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, Payscale for Toronto market data
- LinkedIn Salary Insights for role-specific benchmarks
- Wellfound (AngelList) for startup compensation data
- Consider total comp: base + equity + bonus + benefits
